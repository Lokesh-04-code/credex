# Go-to-Market Strategy — AI Spend Audit

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Primary ICP: Engineering Manager at a Series A–B startup**
- Team size: 5–50 engineers
- AI tool spend: $500–$5,000/month across the team
- Pain: Accountable for tooling budget but doesn't track it rigorously
- Behavior: Reads Hacker News, follows AI tool releases, uses Cursor or Copilot

**Secondary ICP: Technical Founder (CTO/CEO with engineering background)**
- Team size: 2–15
- AI tool spend: $200–$2,000/month
- Pain: Paying for multiple tools out of FOMO, no clear ROI
- Behavior: Active on X/Twitter, listens to Syntax.fm / Changelog podcasts

**Tertiary ICP: Head of Engineering at a growth-stage company**
- Team size: 50–200 engineers
- AI tool spend: $5,000–$50,000/month
- Pain: Board asking questions about AI infrastructure costs
- Behavior: Subscribes to The Pragmatic Engineer, attends LeadDev

## Where Users Hang Out

### Reddit
- **r/ExperiencedDevs** (700k+ members): Engineering leaders discussing tools and process
- **r/startups** (1.5M members): Founders discussing spending and tooling
- **r/SaaS**: Product builders who understand subscriptions
- **r/LocalLLaMA**: Power AI users who care deeply about cost optimization
- **r/cscareerquestions**: Developers conscious of tool costs

**Approach**: Post as a founder sharing a tool you built. "I noticed we were paying for 3 AI coding tools for 4 engineers. Built a quick audit tool — here's what it found." Authentic, value-first.

### Discord
- **Buildspace Discord**: 50k+ builders, very receptive to founder tools
- **Theo (t3.gg) Discord**: Technical audience, opinionated about tech choices
- **Syntax.fm Discord**: Web developers, many using Copilot/Cursor
- **Anthropic Community Discord**: Claude users — core audience
- **AI Engineers Discord**: Infrastructure-focused, cost-conscious

### X/Twitter Communities
- Follow and engage with: @swyx, @benediktmeurer, @rauchg (Vercel CEO), @kepano
- Target hashtags: #AItools #devtools #startuplife #cursor #copilot
- Post type that works: "We audited our AI spend. Found $X/month in waste. Here's what was wrong."

### Hacker News
- **Show HN post**: "Show HN: I built a free 3-minute audit tool for AI tool spending"
- Timing: Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11am ET (peak HN engagement)
- Hook: Lead with the specific problem and the deterministic approach (no AI for math)
- HN users are allergic to hype — emphasize the transparency and open pricing data

## Realistic Traction Expectations

**Week 1** (post-PH launch): 200–500 total audits, 30–80 leads
**Month 1**: 500–2000 total audits, 50–200 leads
**Month 3**: 200–500 audits/week with compounding SEO and social sharing

**Key insight**: Every shared audit URL is a viral loop. If the top 10% of audits are shared publicly (which they will be — high savings users want to show their board), that drives new audits organically.

The tool has inherent virality: sharing a public audit URL to your manager or board requires no extra friction and provides value to the recipient, who then has a reason to audit their own team.
